A Genus That Can Be Predicted for You

Coelogyne

This genus is known for its beautiful, often fragrant flowers, which come in a variety of colors, primarily whites and yellows. Coelogyne orchids are mostly found in the cooler regions of Asia, such as the Himalayas, and are popular for their ease of cultivation.

Dendrobium

One of the largest orchid genera, Dendrobium species are diverse in habitat, form, and flower appearance. They are native to Southeast Asia, the Pacific Islands, and Australia. These orchids are known for their long-lasting flowers, which can be found in a wide range of colors.

Onchidium

Oncidium, popularly known as 'dancing lady' orchids, are famed for their large sprays of small, butterfly-like flowers. These orchids exhibit a wide variety of colors and patterns, with distinctive floral designs. Native to the Americas, they thrive in a broad range of environments, from semi-desert habitats to rainforests. Oncidiums are predominantly epiphytic and are known for their extensive root systems and large pseudobulbs.

Phalenopsis

Commonly known as "moth orchids," Phalaenopsis are among the most popular orchids in the trade, due to their long bloom period and the ease of growing them indoors. They typically have broad, glossy green leaves with long-lasting flowers that come in various colors and patterns.

Vanda

Vanda orchids are known for their large, showy flowers and aerial root systems. They are primarily found in tropical Asia and are popular in horticulture for their vibrant and diverse flower colors. Vandas require a lot of light and air movement and are often grown in baskets to accommodate their root systems.

How to Use the Orchid Prediction Feature

  1. Navigate to the 'Prediction' menu.
  2. Choose between 'Select Image from Storage' or 'Take a Photo'.
    • Selecting 'Choose Image' will redirect you to your gallery or local storage to pick an orchid image for prediction.
    • Choosing 'Take a Photo' will open your camera; aim at the orchid, then click 'Capture Image' or tap the camera screen.
  3. Once the image is uploaded, click the 'Submit' button to start the prediction.
